Quantum Loom — What am I seeing?

An entirely new kind of living visualization: a Klein-bottle phyllotaxis garden woven with temporal threads. Seeds grow on a phyllotactic spiral but are mapped over a rotating Klein bottle in 3D, then collapsed back into 2D. Threads traverse a responsive vector field that you bend with your pointer, and everything is gently diffused into light using a software bloom pass.

Gestures:
- Move your pointer to warp spacetime and attract filaments.
- Click to stamp ephemeral runic glyphs that dissolve into the weave.
- Scroll over the stage to zoom the garden density.

Controls:

  • R — Randomize the seed and regenerate the entire universe.
  • Space — Pause/Resume the simulation.
  • S — Save a high-resolution PNG snapshot.
  • H — Toggle this help.
  • 1..6 — Switch color palettes.

Sliders let you sculpt the number of threads, field warp intensity, temporal speed, bloom diffusion, persistence, and density of the phyllo garden, as well as glyph flux.

The result is a dynamic, never-before-seen field of living ornamentation. It is not a kaleidoscope, not a typical flow field, not a simple particle system—it's a quantum loom weaving color, space, and time.
